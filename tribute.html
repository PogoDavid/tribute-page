Kobe Bryant

Mamba Mentality

  • August 23, 1978: Kobe Bean Bryant is born in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, to Joe Bryant and Pamela Cox Bryant.
  • 1984: Kobe begins playing basketball at the age of six.
  • 1985: Moves to Italy with his family when his father Joe Bryant continues his basketball career in the Italian league.
  • 1991: Returns to the United States and plays high school basketball at Lower Merion High School in Ardmore, Pennsylvania
  • 1995: Named the Pennsylvania Player of the Year and McDonald's All-American while in high school.
  • June 26, 1996: Drafted by the Charlotte Hornets with the 13th overall pick in the first round of the NBA draft, but traded to the Los Angeles Lakers shortly thereafter.
  • 1996: Becomes the youngest NBA player in history at the age of 18.
  • 1997: Wins the NBA Slam Dunk Contest during All-Star Weekend, becoming the youngest player to win the contest at the age of 18.
  • 1999-2002: Establishes himself as one of the NBA's top players, winning three consecutive NBA championships with the Lakers alongside teammate Shaquille O'Neal.
  • 2003: Accused of sexual assault in Colorado; the case is later dropped, and Bryant apologizes to his accuser while maintaining his innocence.
  • 2004: Releases his rap album "K.O.B.E."
  • 2004-2007: Experiences a turbulent period with the Lakers, marked by personal and team struggles.
  • 2006: Scores a career-high 81 points in a single game against the Toronto Raptors.
  • 2008: Wins the NBA Most Valuable Player (MVP) award for the 2007-2008 season.
  • 2008 and 2012: Wins gold medals with the United States men's basketball team at the Summer Olympics in Beijing and London, respectively.
  • 2009-2010: Captures two more NBA championships with the Lakers, earning NBA Finals MVP honors for both championships.
  • 2013: Suffers a season-ending Achilles tendon injury.
  • 2014: Announces his retirement from basketball but later reverses his decisino.
  • 2016: Announces his retirement from professional basketball after a 20-year career with the Lakers.
  • 2018: Wins an Academy Award for Best Animated Short Film for "Dear Basketball", a film based on a poem he wrote announcing his retirement.
  • January 26, 2020: Tragically dies in a helicopter crash in Calabasas, California, along with his daughter Gianna and Seven others.
